Vlad the Impaler

The real Dracula

As the cognomen "The Impaler" suggests, his practice of impaling his enemies is part of his historical reputation.

Important events in Vlad's life:

  • 1431 - Born at Sighisoara, Mures County, Kindgom of Hungary ( today Romania)
  • 1436 - follows his father, Vlad II Dracul,who becomes King of Wallachia and moves to The Royal Court in Targoviste
  • 1442 - by political reasons, he was sent by his father as a prisoner to Sultan Murad II's court toghether with his brother, Radu the Handsome.
  • 1447 - freed imediatly after his father's death.
  • 1448 - accompanied by Turkish army, he is crowned as King of Wallachia, but two months later is defeated by Vladislav II.
  • 1456 - defeats Vladislav II, is crowned a second time and takes revenge on the boyars who killed his father and brother Mircea.
  • 1459 - he arrested all noble families who had taken part in the princely party. The oldest ones were impaled and the others were obliged to walk 100 km from the capital to Poenari.He often ordered that condamned people to be skinned, boiled, beheaded, blinded, strangulated, hanged, burnt, roasted, chopped, nailed, burried alive, or have their organs cut. But his favourite torture was impalement, hence the name. This year he refused to pay the 10.000 coins tribute to the Turks.
  • 1460 - the Turks try to stop his allegience with Matei Corvin, but were unsuccessful.
  • 1461 - 1462 - organised a surprise attack, killing 20.000 Turks
  • 1462 - his greatest blow against the Turks happened, making them retreat before arriving at Targoviste. Later, instead of receiving help from his ally, he is let down by Matei and forced to live at Buda under house arrested.
  • 1475 - is freed by his cousing Stephen the Great, Moldavia's king and was recognised as the true king of Wallachia.
  • 1476 - is murdered and his head is sent to the Sultan, which impales it as a sign of triumph.

The connection of the name "Dracula" with vampirism was made by Bram Stoker around the 1890s. Since then, the character of 'Count Dracula' has recurred through vampire mythology and popular culture.
